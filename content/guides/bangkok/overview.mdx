Here's what actually matters when you're trying to work remotely from Bangkok.

Bangkok is one of the most underrated bases on the planet for digital nomads. The internet is fast, the food is good, the BTS makes the city navigable in flip-flops, and you can live well on £1,200/month if you set up properly.

The catch: a bad setup will ruin the first two weeks. Wrong area, wrong condo, no SIM, no plan for cafes — and suddenly you're losing client calls and walking 30 minutes in 38°C to find a Starbucks with a plug.

This guide stops that from happening.

## What you'll get out of it

* A clear picture of the **best areas** to live in and which to avoid
* A **first 24 hours checklist** that gets you fully set up before bed
* Three honest **monthly budget tiers** with a calculator you can play with
* Real **cafes and coworking** you can work from on a Tuesday morning
* The **WiFi, SIM, and apps** stack that actually works in Thailand
* An honest **scooter reality check** (Bangkok is not where you learn)
* Ranked **weekend trips** so you don't waste a Saturday
* Every **mistake** we've seen first-timers make

## How to read this

<ProTip>
  You don't need to read it in order. The dashboard groups everything into the order most people need it. If you're flying in next week, start with **First 24 Hours** and **Best Areas to Stay**. Everything else can wait.
</ProTip>

## A note on tone

This isn't a tourist blog and it isn't a luxury travel magazine. We're not going to tell you Bangkok is "a magical city of contrasts." We're going to tell you that booking an Airbnb in Khao San Road for a month is a mistake, that not all condo WiFi is actually good, and that you should never trust the first SIM kiosk you see at the airport.

Bangkok is beginner-friendly, but only if you set yourself up properly.

Let's get you set up properly.
