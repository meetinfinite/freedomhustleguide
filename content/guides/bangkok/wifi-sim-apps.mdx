Internet is the difference between Bangkok being one of the best nomad cities in the world and Bangkok being the worst week of your life. It's also the easiest thing to set up — if you know what to do.

## SIM / eSIM

<ProTip>
The cheat code: **buy an Airalo eSIM before you fly**. It activates the moment you land. Then in the first week, walk into an AIS or True shop and buy a proper local SIM with a tourist data package. Best of both worlds.
</ProTip>

### Options ranked

1. **Airalo eSIM (pre-arrival)** — £5–£10 for 7 days, instant. Best for the first week.
2. **AIS tourist SIM (in Thailand)** — ~£10 for 30 days, 30+ GB. Best value, fastest network.
3. **True Move H** — similar to AIS, slightly cheaper, slightly less coverage outside Bangkok.
4. **DTAC** — fine but smaller network footprint.

<WarningCard title="Skip the airport SIM vendors *outside* the terminal" severity="warn">
The official AIS / True kiosks **inside** the AOT terminal are fine. The hustlers waiting outside arrivals will charge 3x. If you're unsure — wait, get to your accommodation, and walk to a real shop.
</WarningCard>

## Condo WiFi

Don't trust it blindly. Some condos have 500 Mbps fibre. Others have a 2018 ASUS router being shared by 40 units.

### Speed test checklist (do this before you fully unpack)

<Checklist
  id="bangkok-wifi-test"
  title="Condo WiFi sanity check"
  items={[
    "Run fast.com twice — morning and evening",
    "Run a Zoom test call (zoom.us/test) and check video quality",
    "Speedtest.net to check upload (you need 5+ Mbps for video calls)",
    "Check the router location — if it's at the front desk, your condo is a dead zone",
    "Ask the building manager what the contracted speed is",
    "Walk to a corner of the unit and re-test — most issues are dead zones, not bandwidth"
  ]}
/>

<WarningCard title="Before you rely on condo WiFi for client calls" severity="danger">
Always have a backup. The single most embarrassing thing a remote worker can do is drop a client call because their building's WiFi crashed at 2pm Tuesday. **Use your phone's 5G hotspot as a backup** — AIS 5G in central Bangkok is genuinely faster than most condo fibre.
</WarningCard>

## Backup internet setup

- Your phone, on AIS 5G, with hotspot enabled.
- Optional: a second prepaid SIM in a pocket hotspot (TP-Link or similar) for redundancy if you're running a team.
- A coffee shop nearby that you know has good WiFi as your 'go to' if everything fails.

## Apps to download before you fly

### Essentials

- **Grab** — taxis, food delivery, payments. Bangkok is unusable without it.
- **Bolt** — backup ride-hailing. Sometimes cheaper, sometimes faster.
- **LINE** — Thailand's WhatsApp. Used for restaurant bookings, massage shops, friends.
- **Google Maps** — works perfectly here. Download offline map for Bangkok before you fly.

### Transport

- **BTS SkyTrain** — official BTS app. Route + fares.
- **MRT Bangkok Metro** — same for MRT.
- **Bolt Food** — food delivery (sometimes faster than Grab Food).

### Money

- **Wise** — by far the best for transferring money home / paying in THB.
- **Revolut** — backup, decent FX.
- **Bangkok Bank app** — if you open a local account (not required for short stays).

### Connectivity

- **Airalo** — eSIM marketplace.
- **Speedtest** by Ookla — test WiFi quickly.

### Nice to have

- **Klook** / **GetYourGuide** — book trips.
- **Klang** (Thai) — restaurant deals.
- **Foodpanda** — backup delivery.

<ProTip>
Set up Grab with your card *before* you fly. Trying to add a card while standing at arrivals with no signal is a special kind of pain.
</ProTip>
