title: Mistakes to Avoid
description: Every mistake we made, so you don't.

You can read every other section of this guide. But honestly — just avoiding the mistakes below will make your Bangkok stay better than 80% of the nomads we've watched arrive.

## Accommodation mistakes

<WarningCard title="Booking too far from BTS/MRT" severity="danger">
You'll Google "Sukhumvit condo" and find something gorgeous for £400/month, then realize it's a 25-minute hot walk to the nearest BTS. You won't go out. You won't go to cafes. You'll order Grab Food and never leave the building. **Rule: under 10 minutes walk from a BTS station, full stop.**
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Assuming all condo WiFi is good" severity="warn">
A condo can advertise "high-speed fibre" and still have a 2018 router serving 50 units. Always book the first 7 nights only. Move if the WiFi is bad. Don't tough it out on a 4-week booking with 12 Mbps.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Staying in Khao San / Sukhumvit Soi 11 if you need deep work" severity="warn">
Beautiful party areas. Terrible work areas. You will hear bass at 2am. The bar district is for nights out — sleep elsewhere.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Overpaying on Airbnb" severity="warn">
For stays over 2 weeks, Airbnb is the most expensive way to book a condo. **Facebook groups ("Bangkok Condo for Rent — Owner") and Hipflat / DDproperty** get you 40–60% off the same units, direct from the owner.
</WarningCard>

## Work mistakes

<WarningCard title="Working from cafes without checking plugs + WiFi first" severity="warn">
Walk in. Order a coffee. Check fast.com and the plug situation BEFORE you settle in. Two hours into a deep-work session is not the time to discover the WiFi gives up at noon.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Not having backup internet" severity="danger">
Your condo WiFi will fail at the worst possible moment. Set up your phone hotspot **before** that moment. AIS 5G in central Bangkok is faster than most fibre — you're covered if you've enabled it.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Booking back-to-back Zoom calls in cafes" severity="warn">
You can take 1, maybe 2 calls in most Bangkok cafes. After that you become the annoying person on a Zoom in everyone's brunch spot. Calls = coworking, condo, or hotel lobby.
</WarningCard>

## City mistakes

<WarningCard title="Underestimating traffic" severity="warn">
A 4km Grab can take 60 minutes in rush hour. ALWAYS check the BTS first. The BTS exists precisely because the roads are unusable at peak times. Use it.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Trying to do too much in week one" severity="warn">
First-time nomads land on Friday and try to see the Grand Palace, eat at Jay Fai, do a temple tour, hit Khao San, and start work all in one week. You'll burn out, get sick, and resent the city. Pace yourself — you're not on holiday.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Drinking out of the tap" severity="info">
Brushing teeth: fine. Drinking: don't. Even seasoned locals don't drink tap water. Bottled water is 7 THB at every 7-Eleven.
</WarningCard>

## Money mistakes

<WarningCard title="Using the wrong ATMs" severity="warn">
**Krungsri** and **Bangkok Bank** ATMs charge ~220 THB per international withdrawal. Yes, it's high. They all charge it. The trick is to **take out more, less often** — withdraw 20,000 THB instead of 4 trips of 5,000.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Not having Wise or Revolut" severity="warn">
Using a normal UK bank card abroad gives you garbage FX rates. Wise and Revolut are essentially free. Set one up before you fly.
</WarningCard>

## Visa mistakes

<WarningCard title="Cutting visa exemption stays close" severity="danger">
Most Western passports get 30/45/60 days visa-free on arrival (depending on country + entry method). **Don't** book a flight that lands on day 31. Immigration fines are 500 THB per day overstayed, plus deportation risk. Check the latest rules for your passport on the official Thai immigration site before booking.
</WarningCard>

## Health mistakes

<WarningCard title="No travel insurance" severity="danger">
Hospital bills in Bangkok private hospitals (Bumrungrad, Bangkok Hospital) are world-class but Western-priced. A motorbike accident without insurance is a £10,000+ bill. Get SafetyWing or similar — £35/month — before you fly.
</WarningCard>

<ProTip>
If you only fix three things from this list: (1) condo near BTS, (2) backup hotspot, (3) Wise card. Those three save more headaches than every other tip combined.
</ProTip>
