You'll be tempted to do every classic Bangkok day trip in your first month. Don't. Some of them are genuinely great, some are overrated, and some are just YouTube content that doesn't actually translate to a fun Saturday.

Honest rankings below. We've actually done all of these.

## In and around Bangkok

<TripCard
  name="Ayutthaya day trip"
  bestFor="History, photos, a real day out of the city"
  timeNeeded="Full day"
  cost="£15–£40"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="The old capital. Stunning temple ruins, easy to do as a self-guided day trip by train (£2) or a cleaner Klook van tour. Go early to beat the heat. Cycle around once you're there — 60 THB bike rental, perfect way to see it."
  bookingLink="https://www.getyourguide.com/"
/>

<TripCard
  name="Damnoen Saduak floating market"
  bestFor="One-time photo experience"
  timeNeeded="Half day"
  cost="£20–£50"
  worthIt="Maybe"
  notes="Famous, photogenic, also extremely touristy. Go once, then you're done forever. Better as a combined tour with Maeklong Railway Market."
  bookingLink="https://www.getyourguide.com/"
/>

<TripCard
  name="Maeklong Railway Market"
  bestFor="A surprisingly cool 30-minute experience"
  timeNeeded="2–3 hours (with transport)"
  cost="£15–£30"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="A market built ON active train tracks. Vendors fold their awnings as the train passes literally 30cm from the produce. Sounds gimmicky, genuinely cool in person."
  bookingLink="https://www.getyourguide.com/"
/>

<TripCard
  name="Bangkok food tour (Chinatown / Banglamphu)"
  bestFor="First-week orientation, foodies"
  timeNeeded="3–4 hours"
  cost="£30–£60"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="Spend the £40 once in your first week. It'll teach you which kinds of street food you like, which stalls to trust, and what to order without a menu. Pays for itself in the meals you'll eat the rest of the month."
  bookingLink="https://www.getyourguide.com/"
/>

<TripCard
  name="Rooftop bars (Lebua / Vertigo / Banyan Tree)"
  bestFor="A nice evening, dates, first-week wow factor"
  timeNeeded="2 hours"
  cost="£20 minimum spend per person"
  worthIt="Maybe"
  notes="Stunning views, expensive cocktails. Go ONCE, dress up (most enforce a smart-casual code), then you've done it. Try Sky Bar at Lebua for the wide view or Vertigo at Banyan Tree for the open-air feel."
/>

<TripCard
  name="Thai cooking class"
  bestFor="Couples, food people, rainy days"
  timeNeeded="4 hours"
  cost="£30–£50"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="Pick one that takes you to the market first (Silom Thai Cooking School, Cooking with Poo). You'll cook 4 dishes and leave knowing how Thai food actually works. One of the best money-spent activities in Bangkok."
  bookingLink="https://www.getyourguide.com/"
/>

<TripCard
  name="Muay Thai event (Rajadamnern / Lumpinee)"
  bestFor="Solo travelers, sports people, anyone doing Muay Thai classes"
  timeNeeded="3 hours"
  cost="£25–£60 ringside"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="Far more intense than expected. Ringside seats are the move — you feel every kick. Skip the package tour markups, just buy tickets at the door."
/>

## Extending the trip

<TripCard
  name="Koh Samet (weekend beach)"
  bestFor="A quick beach reset 3 hours from Bangkok"
  timeNeeded="2 nights"
  cost="£80–£150"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="The easiest 'go to the beach' weekend from Bangkok. Van + ferry, no flights needed. Way less crowded than the southern islands."
/>

<TripCard
  name="Chiang Mai add-on"
  bestFor="Nomads continuing the trip"
  timeNeeded="2+ weeks"
  cost="£100+ flight + £600/month living"
  worthIt="Yes"
  notes="A completely different Thailand. Slower, cooler weather, cheaper, more nature. A natural next stop after a month in Bangkok. Our Chiang Mai guide is coming soon."
/>

<TripCard
  name="Koh Samui / Phuket add-on"
  bestFor="End-of-trip beach reward"
  timeNeeded="1–2 weeks"
  cost="£200+ flight + £1,000+ living"
  worthIt="Maybe"
  notes="Beautiful islands, but the WiFi is worse, the food is touristier, and the prices are higher than Bangkok. Great as a holiday, harder as a nomad base. Save it for last."
/>

<ProTip>
If you only have time for **three** things outside your regular work week, do:
1. The Bangkok food tour (week 1)
2. Ayutthaya by train (week 2)
3. A long weekend at Koh Samet or Pai (week 4)

That's enough Thailand for one month.
</ProTip>
