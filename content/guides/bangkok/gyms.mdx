You will sweat in Bangkok no matter what. You might as well make some of it intentional.

The good news: there's a gym every 300m, a Muay Thai gym in every neighborhood, yoga studios in most upmarket condos, and 60-minute Thai massages for £8.

## How to avoid overpaying

<WarningCard title="Hotel gym day passes are a scam" severity="warn">
A day pass at a 5-star hotel gym in Bangkok runs ~£40. The exact same equipment at Fitness First is a £12 day pass. Unless you're specifically there for a pool, skip it.
</WarningCard>

<ProTip>
For Muay Thai: avoid any gym whose website is mostly in English with stock photos of foreigners punching pads. The good gyms have ugly Facebook pages, photos of actual fighters, and the trainer's mobile number listed instead of an email form.
</ProTip>

## Massage etiquette

- Tip 100 THB for a 2-hour massage if it was good.
- "Strong" in Thailand means strong. Say "soft, please" if you don't want to be folded.
- Don't go to massage places with neon signs in busy nightlife sois. They are not advertising massages.
