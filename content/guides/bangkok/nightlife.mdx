Bangkok at night is a different city. Rooftops with skyline you'd pay £200 a head for in London. Dive bars where Bangkokians actually drink. Night markets that have nothing to do with Khao San Road.

Honest take: nightlife here is huge — but a lot of it is engineered for tourists. This is what we'd send a friend to on a Friday night they actually want to enjoy.

## Going out, the honest version

<WarningCard title="Avoid Khao San if you're staying long" severity="info">
Khao San Road is the experience every first-time backpacker has. Go once, get the bucket cocktail photo, never again. There's better everywhere — including walkable from Khao San itself (try Phra Sumen Road instead).
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Soi Cowboy / Nana Plaza" severity="warn">
Famous, won't lie. But they're red-light districts catering to sex tourism — not "nightlife" in any normal sense. If that's not what you came to Bangkok for, skip entirely.
</WarningCard>

<ProTip>
Bangkok closes the bars at 1–2am officially. After that the unofficial late spots (Wong's, dive bars in Sukhumvit) take over. Don't try to start at midnight — the city peaks at 9–11pm.
</ProTip>

## Getting home safely

- **Grab** is the safest default. Surge prices late, but worth the £4–6.
- **BTS / MRT** stop around midnight. Plan ahead if you're outside walking distance.
- **Motorbike taxis** ("Grab Bike" or orange-vest at the corner) — fastest after 11pm. Wear the helmet.
- Avoid hailing random taxis off the street late — agree on metered fare before you sit down, or walk to the next one.
