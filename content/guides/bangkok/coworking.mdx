Bangkok has dozens of coworking spaces. Most nomads don't actually need one — the cafe scene is too good. But if you're doing daily calls, running a small team, or just hate paying for a flat white every 90 minutes, here's where to look.

## When coworking is worth it

* You're on **3+ Zoom calls per day**.
* You're in Bangkok **2+ months**.
* You want a desk you can leave a laptop at, day to day.
* You want to actually meet other working nomads (cafes are too anonymous).

## When cafes are enough

* You're staying **less than a month**.
* Your work is **deep / async** (no calls).
* You're disciplined and like changing scenery.
* You're on the lowest budget tier.

## How to pick

<ProTip>
  Buy day passes at **two different spaces** in the first week. Almost everyone regrets committing to a monthly without trying it. The vibe difference between WeWork Asoke (corporate) and Hive Thonglor (community) is huge.
</ProTip>

## Cheaper alternatives

* **Workspace Cafe / TomNToon Coffee Library**: cafes that charge by the hour, with plugs at every seat. Closest thing to coworking without the membership.
* **Hotel lobbies**: surprisingly underrated. The lobby at Hyatt Regency Sukhumvit, Park Hyatt, and Sindhorn Midtown all welcome laptops if you order something.
* **University libraries**: Chulalongkorn has a public-access section. Free, calm, good WiFi.

<WarningCard title="Don't book monthly before week 2" severity="warn">
  Most coworking spaces give you steep month-to-month discounts. There's no rush. Day-pass for a week first.
</WarningCard>
