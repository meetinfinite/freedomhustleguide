## What sneaks up on people

<WarningCard title="Bangkok inflation is real for foreigners" severity="warn">
Western groceries (cheese, wine, oats), Western cafes (£4 flat whites), and Sukhumvit nightlife are priced for expats, not locals. You can hit a £3,000/month spend in Bangkok if you live entirely inside a foreigner bubble.
</WarningCard>

<ProTip>
A simple rule: if the menu is in English only, you're paying expat prices. The best food in Bangkok is in places with no English menu and plastic chairs.
</ProTip>

## What you don't need to budget for

- A car (don't).
- A scooter ([see the Scooters section](/guides/bangkok/app/getting-around)).
- A western SIM plan (Airalo + local AIS is plenty).
- A coworking space, if you're disciplined with cafes.
- Bottled water delivery — your condo lobby has it for free or 7-Eleven is fine.
