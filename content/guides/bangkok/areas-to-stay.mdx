Don't choose your area based only on Instagram. The "cool" neighborhoods (Thonglor, Ari) are great if you know what you're getting into. The boring-sounding ones (Asoke, Phrom Phong) are often where you should actually start.

Here's the honest version.

<MapPlaceholder label="Bangkok areas" caption="The eight areas covered in this section, color-coded by nomad-friendliness." />

## How to read these

Every area card gives you the vibe, who it's best for, monthly rent ranges (one-bed condo, mid-range, 30-day stay), the BTS/MRT access, and a **Nomad Score** out of 10. The score weighs WiFi reliability, walkability, work-friendliness, transport, and how easy it is to live there without speaking Thai.

## TL;DR

- **First time, 30 days, just want it easy:** Asoke or Phrom Phong.
- **You like nightlife and good food:** Thonglor or Ekkamai.
- **You want it cheap and local:** On Nut or Ari.
- **You're here to lock in and work:** Phrom Phong, Sathorn, or Ari.
- **Avoid for first stays:** Khao San area, Pratunam, Chinatown.

<ProTip>
Book the first 7 nights only. Walk around. Then book the rest in the area that feels right *to you*. Bangkok neighborhoods feel very different in person than they do in YouTube videos.
</ProTip>
