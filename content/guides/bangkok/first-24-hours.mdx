The first day is where most people lose three days. Wrong taxi, no SIM, no cash, no plan for breakfast — and suddenly it's 48 hours before you've actually opened your laptop.

Here's the order that works.

## Do this first (priority cards)

<WarningCard title="Before you land" severity="info">
  Pre-install **Grab** (taxi/delivery), **LINE** (Thailand's WhatsApp), **Google Maps offline pack** for Bangkok, and **Wise/Revolut**. Buy an **eSIM from Airalo** — you'll have data the second the plane touches down. This single move saves 90% of first-day pain.
</WarningCard>

## Avoid these first-night mistakes

<WarningCard title="Don't take a tuk-tuk from the airport" severity="danger">
  Tuk-tuks at BKK are tourist traps. Use Grab or the official airport taxi queue (yellow stand inside the terminal). Tell the driver to use the meter — say "meter, please" — and pay tolls in cash if asked.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Don't buy a SIM from a random street kiosk" severity="warn">
  You'll pay 3x. Use the **AIS, True, or DTAC** counters inside the terminal — or just rock up with Airalo eSIM and switch to a local SIM after a week.
</WarningCard>

<WarningCard title="Don't drink the tap water" severity="warn">
  Brushing teeth is fine. Drinking is not. Every 7-Eleven sells 7 THB bottles; some condos provide a filter dispenser.
</WarningCard>

## What to actually do on day two

Most people try to do too much. Don't.

* Sleep in. Jet lag is real.
* Find one good cafe near your condo. Just one. Stay 3 hours.
* Walk one new street.
* Eat something memorable for dinner.
* Don't book a tour. Don't go to a temple. Don't try to "see Bangkok" yet.

You're not on holiday. You're moving in. Pace it.

<ProTip>
  The single best move on day 2 is buying a **monthly gym membership** at a place near your condo. It anchors you to a neighborhood routine within 48 hours, which is the fastest way to stop feeling like a tourist.
</ProTip>
